# Persona — Sam & Priya

## Snapshot
- **Household:** Dual income, mid-career professionals
- **Combined income:** ~$180k
- **Budget:** $1.2M (deposit + mortgage capacity, GST and stamp duty included)
- **Children:** None yet, planning in ~3 years
- **Property type:** House OR 2+ bed unit/apartment
- **Tenure intent:** Own-occupy, 7–10 year hold

## Commute pattern
- WFH 3 days/week, in-office 2 days/week
- Office anchor: Sydney CBD (Town Hall used as the proxy point)
- Commute matters but is **not** the dominant criterion — willing to trade ~15 extra minutes for affordability or lifestyle wins

## What "good" looks like for them
| Pillar | What they care about |
|---|---|
| **Affordability** | Median sale price comfortably under the $1.2M budget; ideally room for stamp duty + reno buffer |
| **Commute** | ≤45 min to Town Hall on a typical Tuesday morning |
| **Lifestyle** | Owner-occupier majority neighbourhood, low crime, walkable, eventually school-adjacent |
| **Growth** | Suburb has shown sustained 5-year price growth — they want capital appreciation, not just shelter |

## Default scoring weights
| Pillar | Weight |
|---|---|
| Affordability | 35% |
| Commute       | 20% |
| Lifestyle     | 25% |
| Growth        | 20% |

Weights are exposed as sliders in the Tableau dashboard — the persona defaults above are the saved view, but any user can re-weight to see how the top 5 changes.

## Things this persona explicitly does not optimise for
- School catchment quality (would matter in v2 once kids arrive)
- Bus/light rail commute (proxy is straight-line distance to CBD only)
- Beach/proximity-to-nature lifestyle factors (no data on this in v1)
- Investment / negative gearing — they're owner-occupiers
